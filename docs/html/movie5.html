People

The internet was first created with the intention of enabling research facilities to share their ideas and findings with one another. It took many years for the Internet to be placed in the hands of the common people. Once the Internet was placed into the hands of the common people, common human desires like connection, communication, and love were quickly supported by online websites and apps. Some of these include:

  • Video Sharing (Youtube, Twitch, Vimeo)
  • Email (Gmail, AOL, Outlook)
  • Voice & Video Chatting (Skype, Discord)
  • Dating (Tinder, Bumble, Hinge)
  • Instant Message (Snapchat, Whatsapp)

These apps revolutionized the ways humans communicate and live. Companies and people can stay connected with others no matter place or time and meet new people from anywhere across the world. Here's how each of these message transfer apps changed the world:

  • Video sharing brought content creation to the masses
  • Email gave us a more responsive alternative for physical mail
  • Voice & Video Chatting brought human contact to separated people
  • Dating apps made meaningful relationships between people where they would have otherwise not have existed
  • Instant Messages gave us a new way to maintain relationships and interact with others

With the ability to write or post anything to the web, news no longer came from just the media. Now anyone with a computer could share their stories amd views. Since every story could be heard, some version of the truth can usually be seen at the cost of the blurring of the lines between the truth and lies.

Although these connections open many new possibilities for businesses and connections, they have also been hazardous to our youth. Many of these apps are the reason why internet consumption will likely continue in the future to almost an estimated two and a half hours in 2021. Most social media apps are designed to retain viewers for as long as possible in order to maximize ad revenue. Many developing individuals create unhealthy relationships with social media platforms and even themselves as a result. Hopefully our society is able to adapt and correct these unaccounted issues with the apps that keep us connected.
