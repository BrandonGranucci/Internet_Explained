Dot Com Bubble

Almost immediately after user friendly browser appeared, so did the tech giants that we see today like Amazon and Ebay. These companies soared in value and what followed was a chain reaction. Since new tech companies kept appearing and got funding no matter the idea while telecommunications companies laid fiber optic cables across the country to connect the US. When these companies' stock price kept growing, many companies and people invested in tech companies because of the fear of missing out. Although risky, there were several backings that supported these investments:

  • Moore's Law - Transistors double every 18 months
  • If a bookstore like Amazon (at the time) could be profitable, what couldn't?
  • Metcalfe's Law - Every user increases network's value by much more than 1
  • With so many new companies, there was bound to be another eBay or Amazon with similar returns

With companies like eBay and Amazon, California had a gold rush of programmers trying to get rich off the next big tech company. However, these programmers would get burned along with many companies and retail investors once the bubble burst. The Fed did nothing about the bubble except for raising interest rates once it was too late. The stock market grew fivefold from 1995 to 2000 and then quickly plummeted almost 80%. Although this crash cost the US billions of dollars, there were several benefits from the dot com bubble:

  • Telecommunication companies helped rapidly grow the Internet in 5 years which would have otherwise taken 15 years
  • The crash took care of many directionless companies that were just chasing a quick profit
  • The strongest companies were separated from the weak and became the giants we see today that employ and service millions
